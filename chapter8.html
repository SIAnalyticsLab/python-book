1. Why Visualization Matters

Data analysis is incomplete until results are understandable.

Visualization helps you:

  • See patterns and trends
  • Compare values quickly
  • Communicate findings to non-technical audiences

Good visualization supports decisions. Bad visualization can mislead.

2. Visualization Is About Questions

Before choosing a chart, ask:

  • Am I comparing categories?
  • Am I showing change over time?
  • Am I exploring relationships?

Charts answer questions — they are not decorations.

3. Why Plotly?

Plotly creates interactive charts:

  • Hover tooltips
  • Zoom and pan
  • HTML-ready outputs

Plotly integrates perfectly with Pandas, Quarto, and dashboards.

4. Creating Your First Plotly Chart

import pandas as pd
import plotly.express as px

url = "https://raw.githubusercontent.com/SIAnalyticsLab/30daychallengeguide/main/tips.csv"
df = pd.read_csv(url)

Bar Chart

fig = px.bar(
    df,
    x="day",
    title="Number of Observations by Day"
)
fig.show()

Bar charts are ideal for categorical comparisons.

5. Distributions with Histograms

fig = px.histogram(
    df,
    x="total_bill",
    nbins=20,
    title="Distribution of Total Bills"
)
fig.show()

Histograms reveal spread, skewness, and outliers.

6. Relationships with Scatter Plots

fig = px.scatter(
    df,
    x="total_bill",
    y="tip",
    title="Total Bill vs Tip"
)
fig.show()

Adding a Third Variable

fig = px.scatter(
    df,
    x="total_bill",
    y="tip",
    color="day",
    title="Tip vs Bill by Day"
)
fig.show()

7. Trends Over Time

malaria_url = "https://raw.githubusercontent.com/SIAnalyticsLab/30daychallengeguide/main/malaria.csv"
malaria = pd.read_csv(malaria_url)

fig = px.line(
    malaria,
    x="year",
    y="cases",
    color="country",
    title="Malaria Cases Over Time"
)
fig.show()

Line charts are essential for monitoring trends.

Common Beginner Mistakes

  • Using the wrong chart type
  • Overloading charts with colors
  • Missing labels or titles
